What Is It?
Statement of Intent — Explained
📋 Statement of Intent (SOI)

The Statement of Intent is a short form submitted to MTA before the NEA-RA confirming you plan to serve as a delegate. Think of it as your official RSVP — it tells MTA you're showing up and committed to your responsibilities.

Why does it exist? MTA commits resources before the RA — hotel rooms, caucus space, meals, credentials. They need a firm headcount. If someone doesn't show after MTA has committed on their behalf, that costs money.

What happens if you skip it? MTA will not release any delegate funds — not pre-RA, not post-RA — until both the SOI and W-9 are on file. No SOI = no money, regardless of attendance.

When? Submit it the same day MTA Governance sends the link — typically 6–8 weeks before the RA. It was a JotForm link in 2025. Takes about 2 minutes.

⚡ Bottom Line

SOI and W-9 are a two-key lock. Both must be submitted before MTA releases a single dollar. Don't wait.

Reference
Glossary of Key Terms
NEA-RA
National Education Association Representative Assembly
NEA's highest decision-making body, held annually in July. ~7,000 delegates set NEA policy, elect officers, vote on the strategic plan and budget. 2026: Denver, July 3–7.
MTA Annual Meeting
MTA's state-level delegate assembly. All SEA E-Board members are automatic delegates per SEA Bylaws Art. IV §1(d)(12). 2026: May 8–9, Hynes Convention Center, Boston.
Statement of Intent (SOI)
NEA-RA delegates only — not required for the MTA Annual Meeting. Short form submitted to MTA confirming you will serve as an NEA-RA delegate. Required before MTA releases any NEA-RA funds. Paired with W-9 — both must be on file to unlock reimbursements.
IRS Form W-9
Federal tax form certifying your SSN/EIN. MTA requires it before issuing payment. Submitted twice: to MTA via Adobe Sign, and to SEA via direct email attachment.
Attendance Sheet
Paper sign-in sheet circulated during Annual Meeting business sessions. You must sign it to receive your food stipend for that day. Signing is how MTA and SEA verify your attendance.
Voting Receipt
Receipt generated when you vote during the Annual Meeting. Must be turned in to the SEA office. Confirms you participated in business, not just attended.
Food Stipend
SEA reimbursement for meals during the Annual Meeting. 2026 rates: breakfast $23, lunch $26, dinner $38, incidentals $6.25/day. Total: $184 over two days ($92/day). Requires itemized receipts — no credit card statements, no alcohol.
Mileage Reimbursement
SEA reimburses at $0.70/mile based on your home address. Calculated by the Treasurer's office — you provide miles driven, they calculate the dollar amount. Only delegates who travel to Boston are eligible.
Pre-Convention Meeting
Required briefing before the Annual Meeting. Two versions: MTA's (April 14 or 15, choose one) and SEA's (April 27). Attending both is required to be eligible for the full stipend.
Delegate Funding (NEA-RA)
MTA funding for NEA-RA travel. Local delegates: up to $800, all post-RA. Statewide/Regional: higher amount split pre- and post-RA. Requires meeting the 75% session attendance threshold.
Accountability Requirement
NEA-RA attendance threshold: attend at least 6 of 8 sessions (75%) and at least one session per day (July 4–7). Failing this forfeits the post-RA payment.
MTA Room Block
Hotel rooms MTA contracts at a negotiated rate for NEA-RA delegates. Delegates are required to stay here — staying elsewhere risks MTA penalties and loss of caucus space.
Direct Deposit
Optional setup allowing MTA to transfer reimbursement funds directly to your bank account. Faster and more secure than a mailed check. Set up via Adobe Sign link in MTA Governance email.
SEA Budget Line 60701
SEA's internal budget allocation for MTA Annual Meeting — $10,000 for hotel, $10,062.50 for food and mileage. Separate from MTA's delegate funding. Reimburses from this line after receipt submission.
TIN / SSN / EIN
TIN = Taxpayer Identification Number. For individuals: Social Security Number (SSN). For businesses: Employer Identification Number (EIN). Used on the W-9. Most delegates use their SSN.
PII
Personally Identifiable Information
Sensitive personal data — SSNs, addresses, bank info. W-9 forms contain PII and are handled through a separate confidential email channel, not the general submission form.
